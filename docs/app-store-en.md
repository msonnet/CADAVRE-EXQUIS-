## Description — 4000 car. max

```
One sentence. Several hands. No one sees what the others wrote — until the reveal.

Cadavre Exquis brings the surrealists' famous parlor game — invented in Paris, 1925 — to your pocket. Write a fragment, pass the phone, and discover the poem you made together: absurd, beautiful, impossible to predict.

WRITE TOGETHER
• Three structures: short sentence, Breton's canonical form, free verse
• Choose your visibility: blind, last word only, or the whole previous part
• Hypnotic mode: 30 seconds per fragment — automatic writing, no going back
• Pass the phone around a table, or play online with friends via room codes

SUMMON THE VOICES
• 46 AI voices, each with its own temperament, can take a seat at your table
• The Workshop: a solo séance where you co-write with the voices — without ever rereading your own lines

DRAW THE MONSTER
• The graphic variant: each player draws one band of a body, blind
• The assembled creature is then read by an AI in surrealist verse

KEEP & SHARE
• Your collection lives on your device — no account needed to play solo
• AI illustrations of your poems: watercolor, charcoal, engraving, ink…
• Animated videos ready for Instagram, WhatsApp, or texts
• A public gallery of the community's finest corpses — with a poem of the day

PRIVACY FIRST
• No tracking, no ads
• Solo play stays on your device; online play asks only for a pen name

Every poem is an accident. That's the point.
```
